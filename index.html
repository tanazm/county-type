Counties that are dependent on Mining or Government tend to experience more economic and social problems.

The USDA’s Economic Research Service developed county-level typology codes to provide information about economic dependence and challenges so policymakers, public officials, and researchers could understand what kind of development and public programs each county might need.

Every color on this map represents a different industry. The darker the color, the more challenges the county is facing. The industries include nonspecialized, farming, recreation, manufacturing, government, and mining. The challenges identified are low education, low employment, persistent poverty, persistent child poverty, population loss, and retirement destination.

Click a county to see its name, what industry it depends on, and what challenges it’s facing.
